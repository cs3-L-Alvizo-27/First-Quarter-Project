MY ANNOYING BROTHER
Doo-young (Doh Kyung-soo), a national Judo competitor, permanently loses his sight after injuring his optic nerves during an international competition. In order to get out of prison, Doo-shik, his older brother, who has been alienated from Doo-young (Jo Jung-suk), takes advantage of his brother's sudden problem.

The news that Doo-shik is returning home causes more stress on Doo-young, who lost his parents in an accident when he was a teenager and has had to care for himself ever since. He's having trouble adjusting to the knowledge that he will be permanently blind, and now he has to cope with his con artist brother.

Doo-young, who is first reluctant, gradually warms up to his elder brother, who eventually assumes control and aids him in adjusting to his condition. Doo-shik learns that he is suffering from terminal cancer just as the two brothers are beginning to heal fences. Only a little time remains for him to bid his brother farewell and see him win gold at the Paralympics in Rio, which will guarantee his future.
